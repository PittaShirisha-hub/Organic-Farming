Organic Farming in Orissa

Odisha promotes organic farming via policies like the Odisha Organic Farming Policy 2018 and schemes such as Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY), focusing on sustainable agriculture and the use of bio-fertilizers.

Key organic crops include Kandhamal turmeric (Haladi), Koraput ginger, indigenous rice varieties (e.g., Kalajeera), millets, pulses, vegetables, and fruits, cultivated across various regions of the state.

Highlights

  • Declared “Organic State” with strong political and administrative commitment.
  • Government Support through schemes like PKVY and HAFED for training, certification, and market access.
  • Orissa natural and traditional farming with minimal chemical use, focusing on eco-friendly cultivation of diverse crops in tribal and hilly regions.

Support Programs

Orissa farming through the Organic Farming Mission, PKVY, PGS certification, local organic markets, and farmer training programs.
