Organic Farming in Manipur

Manipur has expanded its organic farming area to 37,900 hectares, a significant increase from just 5,000 hectares in 2016, under the Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER) scheme.

Notable organic crops include Chak-Hao (black rice), which received a Geographical Indication (GI) tag in 2020, and Sirarakhong Hathei chilli, awarded the GI tag in 2021.

Highlights

  • Declared “Organic State” with strong political and administrative commitment.
  • Government Support through schemes like PKVY and HAFED for training, certification, and market access.
  • Manipur natural and traditional farming with minimal chemical use, focusing on eco-friendly cultivation of diverse crops in tribal and hilly regions.

Support Programs

Manipur farming through the Organic Farming Mission, PKVY, PGS certification, local organic markets, and farmer training programs.
